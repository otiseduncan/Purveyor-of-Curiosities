# Purveyor of Curiosities — Brand Foundation

## Brand Name

Purveyor of Curiosities

## Brand Positioning

Purveyor of Curiosities is a curated resale storefront for useful goods, unusual finds, and unexpected treasures recovered from estate cleanouts, storage auctions, rental turnovers, clean-up projects, and forgotten spaces.

This is not a junk store. It is a rotating collection of found, cleaned, inspected, photographed, and listed items given a second life.

## Core Brand Idea

Useful goods. Strange finds. Second chances.

## Tagline Options

Primary:
Curated finds. Useful goods. Unexpected treasures.

Alternate:
Found goods with a second life.

Alternate:
Recovered. Cleaned. Curated. Ready for a new home.

Alternate:
Every item has a story. Some are useful. Some are strange. Some are worth grabbing.

## Brand Voice

The voice should feel:

- Honest
- Curious
- Practical
- Slightly mysterious
- Trustworthy
- Down-to-earth
- Not corporate
- Not gimmicky

## Brand Tone Examples

Use:
"One available. When it’s gone, it’s gone."

Use:
"Found during a local cleanout, cleaned up, photographed, and listed for its next owner."

Use:
"Condition notes are listed clearly so you know exactly what you are buying."

Avoid:
"Luxury resale marketplace."

Avoid:
"Rare priceless treasures."

Avoid:
"Guaranteed authentic vintage estate artifacts."

Avoid:
"Medical-grade", "healing", "treatment", or health claims for soaps, lotions, or personal-care goods.

## Visual Direction

The design should feel like a modern curiosity shop.

Style keywords:

- Vintage-modern
- Warm dark
- Antique labels
- Clean product photography
- Boutique resale
- Archive/catalog feel
- Handmade but professional
- Slightly mysterious but readable

## Logo Direction

The logo should not look cyberpunk, corporate, or generic ecommerce.

Recommended logo concept:

A vintage-style keyhole, magnifying glass, crate stamp, or merchant seal combined with the letter "P" or "PC".

Possible logo symbols:

- Keyhole
- Magnifying glass
- Old shipping crate stamp
- Compass
- Lantern
- Wax seal
- Curiosity cabinet
- Small treasure chest
- Merchant badge

## Typography Direction

Headings:
Use a strong serif or vintage-inspired display font.

Body:
Use a clean sans-serif for readability.

Recommended pairing later:
- Heading: Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond, or Libre Baskerville
- Body: Inter, Source Sans 3, or Manrope

## Brand Promise

We list recovered goods with clear photos, honest condition notes, and practical descriptions so buyers know what they are getting.

## Trust Principles

1. Every item gets clear photos.
2. Every used item gets condition notes.
3. Untested items are labeled as untested.
4. Local pickup items are clearly marked.
5. Personal-care items must be sealed and labeled.
6. Safety-critical automotive parts are avoided at launch.
7. One-off inventory is clearly marked.
8. No exaggerated claims.
